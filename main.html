Falcon 9

"The fast way is to drop thermonuclear weapons over the poles."-Elon Musk/CEO

SpaceX's flagship rocket, capable of sending anything the size of a city bus into geostationary or low earth orbit. It gets its name from its 9 Merlin 1d engines developed by SpaceX. The biggest feauture of the rocket is to land on one of three landing sites. Two landing sites are named in reference of the book "Hitch-Hikers Guide To The Galaxy"; "Of Course I Still Love You" (Atlantic Ocean) and "Just Read The Instructions" (usually Atlantic Ocean but has a further range). The Falcon 9, though currently being fazed out in favour of the Falcon Heavy, is the rocket that SpaceX achieved nearly all of their major milestones with, including; first landing of an OCB, first private company to dock and birth with ISS. It will soon in 2019 be the first private company to send astronauts to the ISS after confirmation of the mission and the crew was made in mid 2018. To hitch a ride on the Falcon 9 it'll set you back about $62M for a full payload.

Three boosters, carrying a payload to orbit, then the rockets land an can be re-used. Well we seen this in February when SpaceX tested the Falcon Heavy with a 95% success. The rocket launched from Cape Canaveral, Florida and landed its two side boosters back on landing pads also in Cape Canaveral, the main stage was to land on the "Of Course I Still Love You" drone ship but missed and made a soft water landing into the ocean.
